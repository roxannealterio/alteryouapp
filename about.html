About

Every woman
starts somewhere.

Combined, we have had the privilege of coaching more than 600 women, with different goals and different bodies, and watching them achieve results they did not believe they had in them.

What I learned

What actually
worked

The opposite of what most women are told. You lift properly. You eat enough, especially protein. If you want to lose fat, we set a real deficit you can actually live on, not the punishing one you have been trying and failing at for years. No crash diets, no fads, no quick fixes. Just genuine accountability and consistency.

And then a few weeks in, you lift something you would have called impossible, and something shifts. You stop asking what your body looks like and start asking what it can do. The fat loss, when that is what you wanted, comes as a side effect of finally doing it in a way you can sustain.

That is why this app exists. To reach as many women as we can, and give them what we found: the results, and the way it feels to finally stop fighting your own body.

Because it was never only about how you look. It is about how you feel.

The honest part

Why I care
about this so much

I should tell you where the anger comes from, because it shapes everything in the app.

I was sixteen when fitness culture nearly took me apart. I got out. It took years, it took help, and it was nothing like a straight line. What I came out of it with was a very specific intolerance for how much of what gets sold to women as health is not health at all.

That is not the whole of me and it is not the whole of this app. Most of the women I have trained have never had an eating disorder. They just wanted to lose weight, or get strong, or be able to carry their kid up the stairs without stopping. All of that is welcome here, and all of it is treated with the same respect.

It just means that when I built this, I knew exactly which shortcuts not to take.

Why the app is like this

Built from real
coaching, not theory

Every decision in ALTER comes from years of coaching real women in person, and seeing what actually works and what quietly holds them back.

  1. 01

    It will never promise a number by a date

    Losing fat is a completely legitimate goal, and ALTER is built to get you there. What it will not do is tell you it takes four weeks. Every program says how long it really takes, before you start it.

  2. 02

    The scale is never the only headline

    Not because your weight does not matter to you. Because if you lift while you lose fat, the number on the scale is the least informative thing in the room, and treating it as the score has broken more women than it has helped.

  3. 03

    There is a Big Mac in the food library

    No food is banned. Not one. A plan you have to break is not a plan, and the moment an app tells a woman a food is forbidden, it has started teaching her the thing I spent years unlearning.

  4. 04

    Your calories cannot be set dangerously low

    There is a hard floor in the code. You cannot get under it, no matter what you type, and no matter how much you want to. This is the single decision I am most certain about.

  5. 05

    It tells you to go heavier

    Most women train for a year and lift the same weight the whole time, because nothing ever tells them to add to the bar. ALTER does, the week you have earned it, and it will not flatter you in the weeks you have not.

  6. 06

    It will never panic you

    No streak about to die. No red badge. No notification at 9pm telling you that you have failed. You are an adult with a life, and the app is a tool, not a supervisor.

If you are where I was

ALTER is a fitness app. It is not treatment and it is not a substitute for treatment. If your relationship with food or your body is hurting you right now, a training app is not what you need first, and I would rather say that plainly than take your subscription.

In Australia, the Butterfly Foundation is free, confidential, and staffed by people who understand exactly what this is. Asking for help was the single best thing I ever did, and it is the only part of my story I would tell you to copy.

Meet your coaches

Meet Roxy and Chey

We met on the gym floor at World Gym, both personally training women face to face. That is where ALTER started: two coaches who had each fought their own battles, watching the same thing play out in front of us every day.

PhotoRoxy Alterio
R

Roxy Alterio

Glute growth & strength

For years my relationship with my body was a war. An eating disorder, a head full of negative self-talk, endless cardio, overtraining and under-eating, and a cycle that left me insecure and exhausted every single day. I remember crying and wondering if any of it was worth it.

Weight training is what turned it around. Fuelling my body instead of punishing it, training to be strong instead of small. Somewhere along the way I fell in love with feeling strong, and I still think it is the best feeling in the world. Nothing else comes close to it.

It made me the happiest and healthiest I have ever been, and it became the whole reason I started coaching women. Every decision inside ALTER comes from that.

PhotoCheyenne Trevatt
C

Cheyenne Trevatt, “Chey”

Recomp & weight loss · retired bodybuilder

I battled eating disorders from fourteen into my early twenties. I lived in fear of food, cycled through fad diet after fad diet, and pushed my body until my health, my energy, my period and my peace of mind all paid for it. So when a woman tells me she feels trapped in that cycle, I am not reading it from a textbook. I lived it.

At twenty-one I decided to change my life. At twenty-two I stepped into bodybuilding, not to punish my body but to finally understand it. Through education, patience and proper nourishment, I rebuilt my relationship with food, with movement, and with myself.

Becoming a mother deepened all of it, and having just had my baby, I am living it right now. Pregnancy, postpartum and breastfeeding have humbled me and taught me to coach a body through constant change, to surrender control, and to respect a body that grows, heals and sustains life. It has given me an admiration for the female body I did not know was possible.

This was never about shrinking yourself. It is about resilience, confidence, and a body that supports your life instead of controlling it. Six years in, more than a hundred women coached, and never once a copy-and-paste plan. Every body responds differently, and yours deserves to be understood.

Between us we have coached more than 600 women in person. We built ALTER to be the app we both needed when we were at our lowest.
