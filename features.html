Nine things

01

It picks your program

Eleven questions. Your goal, your days, your equipment, your injuries. ALTER matches you to one of ten programs and then does something almost nothing else does: it shows you its reasoning.

Why this one. Why not the other nine. If you disagree, you change it in two taps.

  • Foundations, for a first barbell
  • Two Day Minimum, when two is all you have
  • Glute Focus, Home Strength, Bodyweight Base, and eight more

02

It tells you when to lift heavier

This is the whole app. Most women train for a year and lift the same weight the entire time, because nothing ever tells them to go up.

Hit every rep last week? ALTER says go up, and by exactly how much. Fell short? It says hold, and it means it. It will not flatter you.

03

It shows you what wrong feels like

Every form guide has a muscle map, the three cues that matter, and the mistake almost everyone makes.

Not "keep your back straight", which nobody has ever been helped by. Something you can actually feel: if you feel this in your lower back instead of your glutes, your hips are rising first.

form.png
04

Your numbers, not a flat 2,000

Calories and protein calculated from your height, weight, age and how much you actually move. Not the same number handed to every woman regardless of whether she is 45kg or 105kg.

Scan a barcode to log anything packaged. Log in grams, because that is how food is actually sold.

  • No food is banned. Not one.
  • A hard floor under your calories that you cannot set below
  • Protein target you can hit without a shake
fuel.png
05

Training that moves with you

Four phases, and each one changes what ALTER asks of you. Follicular: push, add weight, try the harder thing. Luteal: expect the bar to feel heavier than it is, and know that holding is a win, not a step back.

Every note is about what you can do, never how you look.

cycle.png
06

Progress that is not just the scale

The tape measure, the weights on the bar, the sessions completed. The scale is in there, but it is one line on one chart, and it is never the headline.

Progress photos are stored privately. Only your account can read them. Delete your account and the files are deleted too, not just the database rows.

progress.png
07

The warm up is not a checklist

Press start and ALTER walks you through it, one move at a time. Each one has the setup, what it should feel like, and the mistake almost everyone makes.

When a demo has been filmed it plays right there at the top, looped and silent, so you can copy it without leaving the screen.

  • Twenty moves, each with a real how-to
  • Full body warm up when you are not sure what is coming
  • Or tick them off yourself, your choice
warmup.png
08

Your shop builds itself

Save the recipes you want to cook this week and ALTER turns them into one grocery list. Shared ingredients merge into a single line, and the whole thing is grouped by aisle.

Tick items off as you walk the store. Close the app in the middle of Woolworths and the ticks are still there when you come back.

grocery.png
09

A week you can rearrange

The plan lands on sensible days, but your life is your own. Move any session to any day in two taps. If something is already there, the two swap places, so you never lose a workout.

Sleep and steps sit alongside it, seven days at a glance, so you can see the pattern instead of one number in isolation.
